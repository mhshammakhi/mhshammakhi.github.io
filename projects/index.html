    PhD. in Electrical Engineering, Communication Systems
    School of Electrical Engineering, Amirkabir University of Technology
    Supervisors: Dr. Hamid Sheikhzadeh, Dr. Hamzeh Beyranvand
    Abstract

    In order to improve the quality of fiber-based telecommunications, researchers worldwide are seeking solutions to address the challenges in fiber-optic networks. Accurate estimation of transmission quality (QoT) and signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) plays a vital role in enhancing the performance of fiber optic networks. The presence of linear and nonlinear noise, caused by factors like optical amplifier noise and frequency channel interactions, can degrade the quality at the receiver. To mitigate these destructive effects, it is common practice to assess the quality of transmitters and adjust their parameters based on the network's current conditions. Given the complexities involved in modeling and processing data in fiber optic networks, researchers are turning to artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms. These algorithms assist in adequately training the target model based on network observations. This thesis aims to model both linear and nonlinear noise in various spectral bands and networks. A key objective of this project is to develop a real-time process in the proposed model for optical networks. Therefore, the use of a suitable algorithm with low computational complexity and high accuracy is crucial. In summary, this thesis focuses on addressing the challenges in fiber-optic networks by accurately estimating QoT and SNR, modeling linear and nonlinear noise, and employing a suitable algorithm with high accuracy and low computational complexity for real-time processing in optical networks.

    M.Sc. in Electrical Engineering, Communication Systems
    School of Electrical Engineering, Amirkabir University of Technology
    Supervisor: Dr. Hamid Sheikhzadeh, Advisor: Dr. Vahid Pourahmadi
    Abstract

    This thesis focuses on Bayesian regression methods and introduces two algorithms to enhance sparse Bayesian modeling. The first algorithm aims to reduce complexity, improve computing speed, and handle missing values by leveraging the probability density of data in regression. The second algorithm focuses on improving the variance of the model for test data based on the probability of the test data relative to the probability density of the training data and its distance from relevance vectors. In the first part, a method called RASM (Relevance Artificial Sample Machine) is proposed, which involves sampling from the distribution of the training data to identify potential Relevance Vectors that contribute to constructing a new model. The algorithm is evaluated using four synthetic datasets and one real dataset. The second part introduces HRSFM (Improved Relevance Sparse Feature Machine), a method for enhancing the variance of RVM (Relevance Vector Machine) and RSFM (Relevance Sparse Feature Machine) models. This method addresses the issue of variance reduction caused by moving away from Relevance Vectors in RVM and RSFM. The HRSFM algorithm is tested on an artificial dataset. Overall, this thesis explores Bayesian regression techniques, introduces the RASM and HRSFM algorithms, and evaluates their performance using various datasets.

    Abstract

    The SPECK block cipher, proposed by the U.S. National Security Agency, has garnered significant attention. However, the cipher's security evaluation, particularly its resistance against differential attacks, has not been adequately addressed. Differential attack is a well-known method used against various algorithms, including DES. Therefore, assessing the security of this new cipher against such attacks is of utmost importance. This thesis aims to examine the security of the SPECK block cipher by comparing it with other ciphers in different aspects. Additionally, its efficiency will be evaluated in both hardware and software implementations. Lastly, the functional differences among various SPECK variants, considering key size and block size, will be studied. In summary, this thesis focuses on conducting a comprehensive security analysis of the SPECK block cipher, comparing it to other ciphers, assessing its efficiency, and exploring variations of SPECK with different key and block sizes.
